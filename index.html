About Our Project

The Crediton Cemetery Heritage Portal is dedicated to preserving the rich history and genealogical significance of one of Ontario's historic cemeteries. Established in the 1850s, Crediton Cemetery serves as the final resting place for generations of local families and notable community figures.

Our digital preservation initiative aims to create a comprehensive resource that connects family researchers, historians, and community members with accurate information about those interred here, while preserving the stories and cultural heritage that might otherwise be lost to time.

Historical Timeline

Explore key events in the history of Crediton Cemetery and the surrounding community.

1850s - Establishment

The cemetery was established following the founding of Crediton. The first burial took place in 1853, marking the beginning of the cemetery's history.

1870s - Expansion

As the community grew, the cemetery was expanded to accommodate additional plots. Several prominent community leaders were interred during this period.

1918-1919 - Influenza Pandemic

The cemetery saw an increase in burials during the Spanish Flu pandemic, reflecting the global health crisis's impact on the local community.

1950s - Centennial

The cemetery celebrated its centennial with restoration projects and the publication of a historical record documenting notable burials.

2023 - Digital Preservation Initiative

Launch of the Crediton Cemetery Heritage Portal project to digitally preserve records, create virtual exhibits, and connect with genealogy resources.
